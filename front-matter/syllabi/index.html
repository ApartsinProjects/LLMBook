Course Syllabi: University Programs

How to Use This Section

This section provides four complete 14-week university syllabi. Instructors can adopt a syllabus directly or mix elements from multiple courses. All syllabi assume a 3-hour weekly session (lecture plus lab). Self-directed learners looking for goal-based paths should see FM.2: Reading Pathways instead.

University Course Syllabi

With 36 chapters, no single semester can cover everything. The following four syllabi are designed for instructors adopting this book for a single-semester (14-week) university course. Click any card for the complete week-by-week syllabus with hyperlinked chapter references.

Undergraduate Engineering

Audience B.Sc. Computer Science / Software Engineering students
Duration 14 weeks, 3 hours/week (lecture + lab)
Focus NLP foundations, transformer architecture, LLM APIs, prompt engineering, RAG, building and deploying basic agents

Undergraduate Research

Audience B.Sc. students with strong math background seeking research paths
Duration 14 weeks, 3 hours/week (lecture + lab)
Focus Transformer internals, pre-training, scaling laws, attention analysis, interpretability, alignment methods

Graduate Engineering

Audience M.Sc. / M.Eng. students building production AI systems
Duration 14 weeks, 3 hours/week (lecture + lab)
Focus Full stack from APIs through fine-tuning, RAG, multi-agent systems, evaluation, observability, production deployment at scale

Graduate Research

Audience M.Sc. / Ph.D. students focused on LLM theory and frontiers
Duration 14 weeks, 3 hours/week (lecture + seminar)
Focus Training dynamics, RLHF/DPO alignment, mechanistic interpretability, scaling frontiers, emerging architectures, safety and societal impact
