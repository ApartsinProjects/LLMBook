About the Authors

Alexander (Sasha) Apartsin, Ph.D.

Faculty, School of Computer Science, Holon Institute of Technology

Alexander (Sasha) Apartsin is an AI scientist and educator with three decades of hands-on software engineering, research, and technical leadership experience. He holds a B.Sc. in Computer Science from the Technion (Israel Institute of Technology), an M.Sc. in Computer Science from the Weizmann Institute of Science, an M.Sc. in Management from the Polytechnic Institute of NYU, and a Ph.D. in Computer Science from Tel Aviv University. He completed a postdoctoral fellowship at the Weizmann Institute.

His research focuses on deep generative models applied to NLP, computer vision, and robotics, with a particular emphasis on synthetic data generation for training and evaluation in education, healthcare, and defense sectors where labeled data is scarce. He holds eight patents in machine learning optimization and signal processing.

Before entering academia full-time, Apartsin led data science and research teams across the technology sector, serving as Head of Data Science in automotive AI, Head of Research at a global communications company's innovation center, and AVP of Data Science at a major financial institution's innovation lab. His earlier career spanned two decades in software architecture and product development roles.

At the Holon Institute of Technology, he teaches courses in AI, generative AI, large language models, and computer vision, using an Innovation-First Learning pedagogy that integrates theory and practical tools from day one. He has also advised B.Sc. and M.Sc. students at Afeka College of Engineering for over a decade.

Yehudit Aperstein, Ph.D.

AI/ML Scientist & Educator, Afeka Academic College of Engineering

Yehudit Aperstein is an AI/ML scientist and educator with extensive experience in initiating, managing, and consulting on applied research projects in artificial intelligence, machine learning, deep learning, data mining, and time series analysis. She holds an M.Sc. in Game Theory from the Technion (Israel Institute of Technology) and a Ph.D. in Mathematical Economics from the Weizmann Institute of Science, followed by a postdoctoral fellowship in Financial Mathematics at Bar-Ilan University.

She founded and directed the M.Sc. Program in Intelligent Systems at Afeka Tel Aviv Academic College of Engineering (2016 to 2023), building a graduate curriculum that spans AI, deep learning, reinforcement learning, NLP, multi-agent systems, computer vision, time series analysis, healthcare intelligent systems, autonomous vehicles, and optimization. In 2024 she founded ICSGen.AI, the Afeka Interdisciplinary Center for Social Good and Generative AI, which conducts cross-domain applied research at the intersection of generative AI, healthcare, and social impact. She previously led the Afeka Center for Data Mining and Decision Making (2013 to 2020), directing applied research spanning computer vision, NLP, signal processing, and financial and medical data analytics.

Beyond her academic roles, she runs Applivision Consulting, providing AI strategy and applied research services to industry clients. Her research interests center on robust AI for intelligent systems, generative AI, and their applications to social good.

About This Book

This book was created through a collaborative process between its human authors and a team of specialized AI writing agents. The authors curated every chapter, validated all technical content, and made all editorial decisions; AI agents proposed initial drafts, generated code examples, created illustrations, and checked cross-references across the 36-chapter structure.

The writing process employed 42 purpose-built AI agents, each responsible for a specific editorial function: curriculum alignment, deep explanation, cognitive load management, code pedagogy, fact integrity, style consistency, and more. These same 42 agents also serve as the book's Wisdom Council, contributing the epigraphs that open every chapter. You can meet them and learn about their personalities in the Writing Team roster.

Many of the book's illustrations were produced using Google Gemini's image generation capabilities, with prompts crafted by the authors and refined through iterative feedback. All diagrams and SVG figures were either hand-coded or generated and reviewed for technical accuracy.

The result is a textbook that combines the depth and rigor of expert-authored content with the breadth and consistency that AI-assisted production enables, covering a field that evolves too rapidly for traditional publishing timelines.
