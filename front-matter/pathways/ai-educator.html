Pathway 18: "I Want to Teach AI to Others" (AI Educator / Trainer)
Time estimate: 6 to 8 weeks Difficulty: Beginner

Target audience: Teachers, corporate trainers, and curriculum designers who need to build AI literacy programs or AI-augmented learning experiences

Goal: Develop broad understanding across all major LLM topics, with enough depth to explain concepts clearly, design exercises, and help students build practical projects. This pathway prioritizes breadth over depth.

Approach

Read the overview and "Big Picture" sections of every chapter. Focus deeply on the applied chapters that your students will need most. Use the Course Syllabi (FM.3) as templates for your own curriculum design.

Chapter Guide

  • Focus Ch 00: ML and PyTorch Foundations (core teaching material)
  • Skim Ch 01: NLP and Text Representation
  • Skim Ch 02: Tokenization (great live demo topic)
  • Skim Ch 03: Sequence Models and Attention
  • Focus Ch 04: The Transformer Architecture (key concept for all students)
  • Skim Ch 05: Decoding and Text Generation
  • Skim Ch 06: Pre-training and Scaling Laws
  • Skim Ch 07: The Modern LLM Landscape
  • Skim Ch 08: Reasoning Models
  • Focus Ch 10: Working with LLM APIs (hands-on starting point for workshops)
  • Focus Ch 11: Prompt Engineering (most-requested workshop topic)
  • Skim Ch 12: Hybrid ML+LLM Architectures
  • Skim Ch 19: Embeddings and Vector Databases
  • Focus Ch 20: RAG (popular workshop project)
  • Focus Ch 22: AI Agents (engaging demo material)
  • Skim Ch 27: Multimodal Models
  • Skim Ch 28: LLM Applications
  • Focus Ch 29: Evaluation (teaching students to evaluate their own work)
  • Focus Ch 32: Safety, Ethics and Regulation (essential for responsible teaching)
  • Skim Ch 35: AI and Society
