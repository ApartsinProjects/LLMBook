Cross-Chapter Reading Paths

Goal-oriented tracks that group chapters and sections from across the book into focused learning sequences.

Course and Self-Study Pathways Chapter 00: ML & PyTorch Foundations

If you are reading this book with a specific goal rather than cover to cover, these curated paths group chapters and sections from across the book into focused learning tracks. Each path is self-contained: follow the numbered steps in order, and you will build a coherent understanding of your target area without needing to read every chapter.

Data Engineering Track

Building and curating datasets for LLM training, fine-tuning, and evaluation.

  1. Section 06.4: Data Curation at Scale (how pre-training corpora like FineWeb and Dolma are assembled)
  2. Chapter 13: Synthetic Data Generation (full chapter on Evol-Instruct, self-play, quality filtering)
  3. Section 19.4: Document Processing and Chunking (turning raw documents into structured inputs)
  4. Section 14.6: Fine-Tuning for Classification and Sequence Tasks (data quality requirements for supervised fine-tuning)
  5. Chapter 29: Evaluation, Experiment Design and Observability (benchmarking datasets and measuring model performance)
Agent Builder Track

Building AI agents from API calls through multi-agent orchestration, with production deployment.

  1. Chapter 10: Working with LLM APIs (authentication, streaming, structured outputs)
  2. Chapter 11: Prompt Engineering and Advanced Techniques (chain-of-thought, few-shot, reflection loops)
  3. Chapter 20: Retrieval-Augmented Generation (grounding agents in external knowledge)
  4. Chapter 22: AI Agents: Tool Use, Planning and Reasoning (ReAct, tool calling, sandboxed execution)
  5. Chapter 24: Multi-Agent Systems (supervisor patterns, debate, pipeline orchestration)
  6. Chapter 31: Production Engineering and Operations (deploying agents reliably at scale)
Researcher Track

Understanding LLM internals, scaling behavior, and frontier research directions.

  1. Chapter 04: The Transformer Architecture (self-attention, positional encoding, layer norms)
  2. Chapter 06: Pre-training, Scaling Laws and Data Curation (Chinchilla, power laws, compute-optimal training)
  3. Chapter 08: Reasoning Models and Test-Time Compute (chain-of-thought scaling, verification)
  4. Chapter 17: Alignment: RLHF, DPO and Preference Tuning (reward modeling, constitutional AI)
  5. Chapter 18: Interpretability and Mechanistic Understanding (probing, logit lens, superposition)
  6. Part X: Frontiers: Open Problems and the Road Ahead (active research questions for 2025 and beyond)
MLOps / Production Track

Deploying, operating, and maintaining LLM systems in production environments.

  1. Chapter 09: Inference Optimization and Efficient Serving (quantization, KV-cache, speculative decoding)
  2. Sections 14.1 through 14.3: Fine-Tuning Basics (when and how to fine-tune for your use case)
  3. Chapter 15: Parameter-Efficient Fine-Tuning (PEFT) (LoRA, QLoRA, adapter merging)
  4. Chapter 29: Evaluation, Experiment Design and Observability (LLM-as-judge, drift monitoring, A/B testing)
  5. Chapter 31: Production Engineering and Operations (CI/CD for LLMs, guardrails, cost management)
  6. Chapter 32: Safety, Ethics and Regulation (red-teaming, compliance, responsible deployment)
  7. Section 33.4: LLM Vendor Evaluation and Build vs. Buy, Section 33.5: Compute Planning and Infrastructure
Domain Specialist Track

Applying LLMs to domain problems (medical, legal, business, education) without requiring deep ML expertise.

  1. Chapter 00: ML and PyTorch Foundations (skim for vocabulary; skip the code if needed)
  2. Chapter 10: Working with LLM APIs (the practical starting point for using LLMs)
  3. Chapter 11: Prompt Engineering and Advanced Techniques (getting high-quality outputs for your domain)
  4. Chapter 20: Retrieval-Augmented Generation (connecting LLMs to your organization's data)
  5. Chapter 28: LLM Applications (code generation, summarization, search, recommendations)
  6. Chapter 33: LLM Strategy, Product Management and ROI (making the business case, vendor selection, cost planning)
